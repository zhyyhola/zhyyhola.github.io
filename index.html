Welcome!

Welcome to my personal website! I am Yuanyuan Zhang, a fourth-year graduate student at Boston University, specializing in syntax and semantics. My research explores the fascinating interplay between language structure and meaning.

Research Highlights

  • Degree Semantics in Mandarin Chinese: I investigate how degree expressions (e.g., adjectives, degree adverbs) are represented in Mandarin Chinese, focusing on their syntactic and semantic properties. My research highlights cross-linguistic similarities and differences in the degree systems of Mandarin and other languages.
  • Modality in Central Plains Mandarin: My work examines the modality system in Central Plains Mandarin, including dynamic modality (ability), deontic modality (obligation), and epistemic modality (possibility). By combining fieldwork and semantic analysis, I aim to provide a cross-dialectal perspective on modality expressions in Chinese.

Beyond Research

Outside of academia, I draw inspiration from nature and traditional Chinese culture. From making hearty soups to collecting leaves on autumn walks, I find joy in the little things that connect us to the world.
